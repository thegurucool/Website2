The Framework

The TEACH-AI framework

TEACH-AI is thegurucool's proprietary competency framework for K-12 teacher AI readiness, built around seven domains and a multi-level proficiency scale. It was developed to give teachers a way to measure, and build, their ability to teach well in an AI-integrated classroom, not just their ability to use AI tools.

TEACH-AI is not affiliated with TeachAI.org, the CoSN-backed initiative, or any AI competency framework published in academic literature. thegurucool's TEACH-AI is our own framework, built specifically for K-12 classrooms in Southeast and South Asia.

What it measures

Most AI training for teachers stops at tool literacy. It shows you how to prompt a chatbot or generate a worksheet, then calls that professional development. TEACH-AI starts from a different premise: knowing how to use an AI tool and knowing how to teach well with one are not the same skill.

The framework breaks that second skill into seven domains, each measured against a proficiency scale. A teacher does not start at zero. The diagnostic places you at the level that reflects where your practice actually is, then builds a pathway from there.

Seven domains
T
Technology Integration
Knowing which AI tools belong in your classroom, and the judgment to tell the difference.
E
Ethics and Responsibility
Bias, data privacy, and what responsible AI use looks like when the people affected are children.
A
Assessment Design
Building assessments that hold up when students have access to tools that used to be called cheating.
C
Curriculum and Content
Using AI to plan and build lessons faster, without losing the judgment that makes the lesson good.
H
Human-AI Collaboration
Working alongside AI without becoming dependent on it, and without being afraid of it either.
A
Adaptive Teaching
Personalising learning at a scale that reaches the students who used to fall through the gaps.
I
Inclusion and Equity
Making sure AI use in the classroom makes things more equitable for every student.
Grounded in pedagogy

Four decades of research, applied.

TEACH-AI is not a new theory of teaching. It applies four established, well-researched pedagogical bases to the specific problem of AI-integrated K-12 classrooms.

TPACK
Technology Integration · Curriculum and Content
Universal Design for Learning
Inclusion and Equity
Hattie's Visible Learning
Adaptive Teaching
Bloom's Taxonomy
Assessment Design

Ethics and Responsibility and Human-AI Collaboration draw on all four bases together. Both are less about a single instructional technique and more about the judgment that sits above every technique a teacher uses.

Common questions
What is the TEACH-AI framework? +
TEACH-AI is thegurucool's proprietary framework for K-12 teacher AI readiness. It covers seven domains, each scored against a proficiency scale, and is used to diagnose a teacher's starting point and build a personalised path from there.
Is TEACH-AI the same as TeachAI.org? +
No. TeachAI.org is a separate initiative. TEACH-AI is thegurucool's own framework, built specifically for K-12 classrooms in Southeast and South Asia.
What research is TEACH-AI built on? +
TEACH-AI draws on four established bases: Bloom's Taxonomy, TPACK, Universal Design for Learning, and Hattie's Visible Learning. These are applied specifically to AI-integrated K-12 teaching, not repackaged as something new.
Do teachers start at the same level? +
No. The diagnostic places each teacher at the proficiency level that matches their actual practice, then builds their pathway from there.
